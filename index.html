About Me

My name is Khader Khoudeda, and I am a Computer Science student at the University of Nebraska–Lincoln. My journey into the world of technology and software development began in college, where I discovered my passion for coding through various projects and coursework. Throughout my academic journey, I've developed skills in various programming languages and technologies, starting off with python, and eventually stemming out into HTML, CSS, JavaScript, React, Node.js, C++, C#, .NET, SQL, Git, and more. I love solving complex problems and building innovative web applications that offer a great user experience. Beyond my technical skills, I'm a resilient and driven individual who is always eager to learn and grow. I'm committed to making a positive impact through my work and contributing to projects that drive meaningful change. When I'm not coding, I enjoy exploring new technologies, collaborating with peers, working out, and playing basketball. I'm always looking for opportunities to expand my knowledge and take on new challenges. In addition to my academic and professional pursuits, I am passionate about community involvement and mentorship. I believe in the power of sharing knowledge and helping others achieve their goals. Whether it's through simple conversation, or a facilitated meeting, I strive to give back to the community that has supported my growth. My ultimate goal is to leverage my skills and experiences to create software solutions that make a difference in people's lives. I am excited about the future of technology and look forward to contributing to its advancement.
